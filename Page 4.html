As an Axe Master, it is my job to teach people how to throw safely, how to keep score, and proper technique. For this, we will be using the IATF ruleset. On the floor, there is a yellow line that divides the players from the crowd. Axes do not go past the crowd area, drinks and food do not go into the axe arena. After that is the blue line, which is referred to as the "Big Axe" line. This is for Big Axe throwing, which is what we use for tie-breakers. After that is the "black box," which is a reference point. Beyond the black box is the red fault line. Players should never cross that line until their opponent has thrown and all axes are at rest on the ground or in the wood. Each game consists of 3 rounds of 5 throws for a total of 15 throws per game. After each round, you switch sides. For the target, there is the blue line (1 pt), the red line (3 pts), and the black bullseye (5 pts). On your 5th throw of every round, you have the option to go for clutch, which is one of two green dots sitting above the left and right sides of the target. Clutches are worth 7 pts, and have two conditions. 1. You MUST call clutch if you are going for it. 2. Everything else on the board is void; only the clutches count for points. Clutches are a fun, high-stakes way of playing catch-up with an opponent, and a way to flex on your friends. You can go for clutch even if you're winning to seal it up.
